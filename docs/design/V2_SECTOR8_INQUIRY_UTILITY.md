# THROHI V2 — Sector 8 Inquiry and Utility System

## Purpose

Sector 8 moves the website from expressive catalogue exploration into calm, trustworthy task completion. The inquiry and supporting routes use the same THROHI identity with less theatre, more light, softer controls, and clearer evidence boundaries.

## Inquiry principles

- The workflow is an inquiry, never a cart or checkout.
- Products, codes, quantities, notes, manual references, buyer fields, consent, and attachment metadata remain visible and preserved.
- Empty state always offers catalogue and manual-item recovery.
- Manual items are labelled as unlisted rather than presented as catalogue records.
- Failure messaging explains that data remains available.
- Success does not promise a response time.
- Development storage limitations remain visible.
- Duplicate-submission protection remains unchanged.

## Visual hierarchy

- Pale working surface for forms and review blocks.
- Dark green summary panel for final submission context.
- Small professional radii rather than hard-edged interface panels.
- Green confirms active and verified states.
- Blue remains technical.
- Orange appears only for warning, manual, or failure context.
- Display typography is intentionally smaller than homepage and catalogue theatre.

## Supporting routes

### Company

Uses an evidence-first structure. Unverified age, reach, facilities, certifications, capabilities, OEM services, exports, and market claims remain unpublished.

### Resources

Documents appear only when their file, metadata, and route are approved. Pending divisions retain direct catalogue access and never show fake download controls.

### Contact

The structured inquiry is the recommended route. No placeholder email, phone, WhatsApp, address, business hours, or response promise is published.

### Privacy and terms

Legal pages use a narrow reading measure, calm typography, and explicit working-document status. They describe implemented boundaries only and remain subject to legal review.

### Failure routes

404 and application-error pages offer recovery through search, products, inquiry, and retry. Saved inquiry data is not intentionally cleared.

## Protected behavior

Sector 8 does not change:

- inquiry provider storage
- quantity and note updates
- remove and undo
- manual-item creation
- attachment metadata validation
- buyer validation rules
- consent rules
- API payloads
- submission token behavior
- duplicate prevention
- catalogue, search, or migration logic

## Validation

Sector 8 requires desktop and mobile browser coverage for empty inquiry, populated inquiry, quantity and note persistence, remove and undo, manual items, validation errors, server failure preservation, success clearing, utility routes, accessibility, and overflow.
