# THROHI V2 — Sector 6 Catalogue System

## Purpose

Sector 6 translates the approved V2 homepage identity into product discovery without turning the catalogue into another theatrical scroll experience. The catalogue remains faster, calmer, and more task-oriented than the homepage.

## Products landing

- Direct search is the primary procurement route.
- Four divisions remain visible as large editorial rows.
- Product families form a compact scalable index.
- Representative products use object-led cards rather than conventional ecommerce tiles.
- Manual inquiry recovery remains visible near search and at the end of the page.
- Seed data is always identified honestly.

## Division template

- Each division receives a restrained color atmosphere.
- The opening remains shorter than the homepage.
- Search, family routes, product previews, manual recovery, and documents remain direct.
- Family routes use large typography but stay below the revised size ceiling established in Sector 4.

## Family listing

- Desktop uses a compact sticky filter panel.
- Mobile uses a native `details` disclosure instead of a JavaScript drawer.
- Query and sort state remain URL-driven and server-rendered.
- Active division, family, query, and sort context remain visible.
- Result counts and no-result recovery remain explicit.
- Every product remains a normal link.

## Product cards

- Product codes are structural visual elements.
- Placeholder media remains visibly labelled.
- Cards use a large object stage, clear name, seed status, details route, and inquiry state.
- No prices, stock labels, ratings, urgency, or unverified specifications appear.

## Product detail

- The layout behaves like an examination table, not an ecommerce page.
- The product image stage is dominant on desktop and appears first on mobile.
- Product name, code, approved information, quantity, note, and inquiry action remain clearly separated.
- Only verified specifications may publish.
- Document availability is conditional.
- Related products remain direct comparison routes.

## Responsive rules

- Two-column product grids become single-column on mobile.
- Sticky elements become static below tablet widths.
- Filters collapse through native disclosure behavior.
- Buyer task order on mobile is image, identity, code, description, verified facts, inquiry, documents, and related products.
- No horizontal catalogue interaction is required.

## Motion rules

- Only small hover, focus, and state transitions are used.
- Reduced-motion mode removes decorative movement.
- No pinned catalogue narratives, smooth scrolling, 3D, or scroll hijacking.

## Protected behavior

Sector 6 does not change:

- catalogue records
- search ranking
- exact or partial code matching
- filter semantics
- sort semantics
- product routes
- inquiry persistence
- quantity and note behavior
- remove and undo
- API validation
- catalogue migration pipeline

## Honest placeholder rules

- Product and family visuals are replaceable design placeholders.
- Placeholder graphics do not imply material, dimensions, finish, certification, availability, or manufacturing capability.
- Final images must supply meaningful alternative text and responsive dimensions.
