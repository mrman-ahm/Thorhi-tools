# THROHI Website — Project Brief

**Status:** Working foundation approved for wireframing  
**Date:** 2026-07-22  
**Project:** Complete redesign of throhi.com  
**Primary mode:** B2B product catalogue and inquiry website

---

## 1. Project purpose

Create a modern international website for THROHI Medical Tools that presents its instrument range with professional credibility, allows buyers to find products quickly, and converts interest into organized business inquiries.

The website should feel visually memorable and technologically current, but product discovery, trust, speed, and inquiry usability must remain more important than visual spectacle.

The legacy website is a catalogue source only. It is not a visual, structural, technical, or copywriting reference.

---

## 2. Current understanding of THROHI

The available public catalogue presents four main divisions:

1. Dental Instruments
2. Surgical Instruments
3. Beauty Instruments
4. Veterinary Instruments

The existing website contains a substantial number of product families, product names, images, and product codes. It also contains outdated presentation, unfinished placeholder text, inconsistent naming, spelling problems, duplicate or questionable product entries, and unsupported marketing claims. All catalogue information must therefore be cleaned and validated before final migration.

Known public contact information currently includes a Sialkot address, telephone number, email address, and WhatsApp access. These details must be reconfirmed before launch.

---

## 3. Primary business objective

Turn the website into a credible B2B sales and product-discovery channel.

The primary commercial outcome is:

> A qualified buyer finds relevant instruments and submits a clear, organized inquiry.

Secondary outcomes:

- Communicate the breadth of THROHI's catalogue.
- Improve international perception of the company.
- Make product codes easy to search.
- Reduce friction in multi-product inquiries.
- Give sales staff better inquiry information.
- Provide downloadable catalogues and business documents.
- Support future SEO and campaign landing pages.

---

## 4. Target audiences

### Primary audiences

- International importers
- Medical and dental distributors
- Surgical-instrument wholesalers
- Procurement professionals
- Private-label or OEM buyers, only if such services are confirmed
- Veterinary-equipment suppliers
- Beauty-tool and salon-product buyers

### Secondary audiences

- Clinics and hospitals researching products
- Dental practices or groups
- Resellers
- Trade-show visitors
- Existing clients seeking product codes or catalogue references

### Audience behavior assumptions

Buyers may arrive in two distinct modes:

1. **Known-item mode** — they already know a product name or code.
2. **Exploration mode** — they need to browse by division, specialty, family, or use.

The website must support both immediately.

## 5. Primary user actions

### Primary actions

- Submit a multi-product request

### Secondary actions

- Browse product families
- Download a catalogue
- Review company information

- Contact THROHI through email, phone, or WhatsApp

## 6. Strategic design position

The visual system will combine three controlled modes:

### Steel After Dark

Used for the homepage hero and selected brand moments. Dark carbon-blue surfaces, controlled instrument lighting, subtle blue-green reflections, and restrained cinematic motion.

### Surgical Light

Used for the catalogue, product pages, forms, resources, and most content. Bright, precise, highly legible, and optimized for professional browsing.

### Precision Archive

Used for technical annotations, product-family transitions, catalogue references, instrument silhouettes, measurements, and the “Precision Through Time” editorial experience.

### Intended emotional response

- Precision
- Seriousness
- Confidence
- Technical control
- Crafted quality
- Modern capability

### Explicitly rejected directions

- Generic medical template
- Generic SaaS landing page
- Gaming-style neon
- Random 3D objects
- Effects in every section
- Entirely dark catalogue
- Glassmorphism-heavy interface
- Fake dashboards or statistics
- Unsupported claims of superiority
- Local competitor imitation

## 7. Brand color policy

The existing logo remains the identity source, but its current gradients and bright tones will not be spread across every website surface.

Working palette direction:

- Deep surgical navy
- Refined instrument blue
- Precision green
- Carbon ink
- Brushed-steel grey
- Sterile off-white
- Minimal red retained only where necessary within the existing mark

The final palette will be measured and tested after a better logo source is obtained.

The supplied logo is too low-resolution for final production and requires a clean vector reconstruction or original artwork.

The red cross-like symbol inside the wordmark must not be extracted or used as a standalone decorative icon. Legal and trademark review is recommended before any logo reconstruction.

## 9. Core functionality

### Product catalogue

Every product should support:

- Product name
- Product code
- Division
- Product family
- Product images
- Short factual description
- Available variants or sizes, when confirmed
- Relevant catalogue reference
- Inquiry action
- Related products based on real taxonomy

### Search

Search must support:

- Exact and partial product codes
- Product names
- Common spelling variations
- Instrument families
- Divisions
- Relevant specialties or uses, when verified

### Filtering

Filtering should be progressively introduced based on available data:

- Division
- Product family
- Instrument type
- Specialty or use
- Size or variant
- Material or finish
- Availability of catalogue sheet

Only filters supported by reliable data should be shown.

### Inquiry builder

Buyers should be able to:

- Add multiple products
- Change quantity
- Remove products
- Add item-specific notes
- Add general requirements
- Enter company and contact information
- Select preferred contact method
- Attach a reference file
- Submit one organized inquiry
- Receive a clear confirmation

The system must preserve the inquiry basket while the buyer continues browsing.

---

## 10. Content policy

All public copy must be factual, specific, and human.

Do not publish unverified claims such as:

- Leading manufacturer
- World-class quality
- Globally trusted
- Best quality
- Certified
- International standards
- Superior steel
- Decades of experience
- Exporting worldwide

These statements may be used only after the client provides evidence and approval.

Temporary content should use neutral wording such as:

> Explore THROHI's available surgical, dental, veterinary, and beauty instrument catalogue.

---

## 11. Technical direction

The exact stack will be decided after the repository and hosting environment are known.

The implementation should support:

- Structured product data
- Fast server-rendered or statically generated public pages
- Searchable product codes
- Responsive images
- Accessible navigation and forms
- Inquiry persistence
- Secure server-side form processing
- Spam and abuse protection
- SEO metadata
- Analytics with consent where required
- Maintainable catalogue updates
- Production monitoring
- Backups and exportable product data

A modern framework is acceptable only if it improves catalogue maintainability, performance, and deployment. The project should not adopt a complex stack merely for prestige.

---

## 12. Accessibility and performance targets

- WCAG 2.2 AA target
- Complete keyboard navigation
- Visible focus states
- Reduced-motion support
- Responsive behavior from narrow mobile to large desktop
- No critical content hidden inside animation
- No scroll hijacking
- Optimized product imagery
- Performance budget for any 3D or cinematic hero
- Functional static fallback for low-power devices
- No heavy effects on catalogue and product pages

---

## 13. Success criteria

The first version succeeds when:

- A buyer can find a known product code quickly.
- A buyer can explore a division without facing an enormous unstructured list.
- Multiple products can be added to one inquiry.
- The inquiry clearly reaches THROHI with product and buyer details.
- Mobile browsing feels intentionally designed.
- The website communicates a premium international standard without inventing facts.
- The design is recognizably THROHI rather than a template.
- Product data can be maintained without rebuilding pages manually.
- All important loading, empty, error, and success states exist.
- The production site passes build, accessibility, responsive, security, and critical-flow review.

## 14. Main risks

### Product data quality

The legacy catalogue contains inconsistent spelling, duplicate codes, broad category names, and incomplete product descriptions.

**Response:** Create a structured migration sheet and validation workflow before final import.

### Missing business evidence

Important claims about materials, quality, manufacturing, certifications, OEM work, and export history are not confirmed.

**Response:** Keep these areas modular and unpublished until evidence is supplied.

### Effect expectations

The owner may expect a heavy visual-effects website.

**Response:** Deliver one exceptional hero moment and one secondary signature interaction while keeping the catalogue bright and fast.

### Asset quality

The current logo and some legacy product images may be unsuitable for premium presentation.

**Response:** Reconstruct the logo, audit product imagery, define consistent image treatment, and identify priority products for new photography or rendering.

### Catalogue scale

The existing taxonomy is too large and inconsistent for a conventional navigation menu.

**Response:** Use division hubs, grouped families, search, filters, and a structured product database.
