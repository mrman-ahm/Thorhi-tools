# THROHI Website — Homepage Information Architecture

**Status:** Approved structure for low-fidelity wireframing  
**Date:** 2026-07-22

---

## Homepage objective

The homepage must perform three jobs in order:

1. Create a strong premium first impression.
2. Help visitors find instruments immediately.
3. Move qualified buyers toward an organized inquiry.

The homepage is not a long corporate brochure and not an effects demo.

---

## Section 0 — Global header

### Desktop

Left:

- THROHI logo

Center or right:

- Products
- Company
- Resources
- Contact

Actions:

- Search
- Inquiry basket with item count

### Mobile

- Logo
- Search
- Inquiry basket
- Menu

### Behavior

- Transparent or dark over the hero
- Transitions to a solid readable surface after scrolling
- Keyboard accessible
- No oversized navigation animation
- Inquiry count persists
- Search opens instantly

---

## Section 1 — Cinematic precision hero

### Purpose

Create the owner's desired “wow” moment while establishing the product category and giving immediate access to browsing.

### Composition

- Deep carbon-blue background
- One hero instrument or carefully arranged instrument pair
- Controlled moving light and reflective steel treatment
- Minimal copy
- No stock operating-room scene
- No random 3D object

### Working content structure

Eyebrow:

> THROHI Medical Tools

Headline:

> Precision, brought into focus.

Supporting line:

> Explore surgical, dental, veterinary, and beauty instruments by category or product code.

Primary action:

- Explore Instruments

Secondary action:

- Build an Inquiry

Utility:

- Search by product name or code

### Motion

- Slow lighting response or restrained object movement
- Immediate fallback image
- Reduced-motion mode
- No scroll hijacking
- No delay before controls become usable
- Mobile version should use lighter motion or a static cinematic composition

---

## Section 2 — Product search and known-code route

### Purpose

Serve buyers who already know what they need.

### Content

Heading:

> Find an instrument

Search placeholder:

> Search by product name or code

Helpful examples should use real validated codes later.

### Results behavior

- Instant suggestions
- Exact-code priority
- Product thumbnail
- Product name
- Product code
- Division and family
- Open product
- Add to inquiry

This utility may visually overlap the bottom of the hero on desktop, but it must remain highly legible and stable.

---

## Section 3 — Four product divisions

### Purpose

Give exploration-mode buyers immediate orientation.

### Entries

- Surgical Instruments
- Dental Instruments
- Veterinary Instruments
- Beauty Instruments

### Visual direction

Each division uses:

- A distinctive real instrument silhouette or macro photograph
- Clear title
- One-line factual description
- View division action
- Number of families only if the data is accurate

### Interaction

- Subtle lighting or image shift
- No unrelated icon set
- No identical generic cards
- On mobile, use a deliberate horizontal or stacked composition with clear touch targets

---

## Section 4 — Explore by instrument family

### Purpose

Expose the breadth of the catalogue without showing an unmanageable category list.

### Content

A curated set of common families such as:

- Scissors
- Forceps
- Retractors
- Needle Holders
- Dental Extraction
- Curettes and Scalers
- Hoof and Farrier
- Manicure and Pedicure

The exact set must be selected from validated catalogue data.

### Design

- Bright Surgical Light surface
- Product silhouettes, cropped photography, or technical linework
- Compact but not crowded
- Search and “View all families” remain available

---

## Section 5 — Signature precision interaction

### Purpose

Deliver a second memorable moment that is directly connected to the instruments.

### Concept

A macro instrument close-up with controlled annotations.

Possible annotated areas:

- Working end
- Joint
- Handle
- Surface
- Product code location

Only verified descriptions should be used.

### Interaction

- Desktop pointer or scroll reveals details
- Mobile tap reveals one detail at a time
- No fake technical measurements
- Reduced-motion version displays the same information statically

This section should demonstrate attention to form and craftsmanship without claiming unsupported manufacturing processes.

---

## Section 6 — Product discovery preview

### Purpose

Show that the website is a real catalogue, not only a brand presentation.

### Content

- Recently selected or strategically chosen products
- Product name
- Product code
- Division or family
- Add to inquiry
- View details

### Selection rule

Do not call products “best sellers” or “featured” unless the client confirms the basis. Use neutral labels such as:

- Explore selected instruments
- From the catalogue
- Browse by family

---

## Section 7 — Precision Through Time

### Purpose

Create a distinctive editorial brand story around the evolution of instrument form.

### Content direction

- Historical form progression
- Scissor or instrument evolution
- Material and mechanical milestones from credible sources
- Modern form conclusion leading back to the catalogue

### Important boundary

This is an industry and design-history story. It must not imply that THROHI personally invented historical developments or has operated throughout the entire timeline.

### Interaction

- Controlled horizontal or vertical progression
- No forced long scroll sequence
- Accessible text equivalent
- Separate full experience available through Resources

---

## Section 8 — Inquiry builder introduction

### Purpose

Explain the website's main conversion tool.

### Content

Heading:

> Build one inquiry from multiple products.

Supporting explanation:

> Add instruments as you browse, specify quantities, and send your requirements together.

Actions:

- Start an Inquiry
- Review Selected Products

### Visual

- Clean bright layout
- Real product rows or basket interface
- No fake dashboard
- Clear three-step process at most

---

## Section 9 — Company credibility

### Purpose

Answer “Who is THROHI?” without publishing unsupported claims.

### Initial safe content

- Company name
- Location
- Product divisions
- Direct business contact
- Link to About

### Future expandable evidence

Only after confirmation:

- Manufacturing capabilities
- Quality processes
- Materials
- Certifications
- Export regions
- Customization
- Factory imagery

### Rule

Evidence should replace generic words. One real factory image or verified certificate is more useful than several vague quality claims.

---

## Section 10 — Catalogues and resources

### Purpose

Serve buyers who prefer conventional PDF catalogues or product documents.

### Content

- Main catalogue
- Division catalogues
- Update date
- File type and size
- Browse digital catalogue action

Do not display empty download cards while documents are unavailable.

---

## Section 11 — Contact conversion band

### Purpose

Provide a strong final action without repeating the whole homepage.

### Content

Heading:

> Looking for a specific instrument?

Actions:

- Search Products
- Send an Inquiry
- Contact THROHI

Include approved email, phone, and WhatsApp.

---

## Section 12 — Footer

Contains:

- Product divisions
- Company links
- Resources
- Inquiry
- Contact
- Legal
- Social links only where active and approved

No unsupported compliance logos.

---

## Homepage content priority by device

### Desktop

1. Hero impact
2. Search
3. Product divisions
4. Catalogue breadth
5. Signature interaction
6. Inquiry conversion
7. Brand story and resources

### Mobile

1. Clear identity
2. Search
3. Product divisions
4. Inquiry basket access
5. Common families
6. Catalogue products
7. Reduced signature interaction
8. Contact

The mobile homepage should not require passing through a long cinematic story before reaching products.

---

## Homepage visual ratio

- 25–30% dark cinematic surfaces
- 70–75% bright functional surfaces
- One primary cinematic effect
- One secondary signature interaction
- All remaining motion limited to feedback, hierarchy, and continuity

---

## Homepage wireframe acceptance criteria

The wireframe is ready for visual design when:

- Search is visible without hunting.
- Four divisions are understandable immediately.
- The primary inquiry route is repeated without becoming aggressive.
- No section exists only to fill space.
- Each section has one clear responsibility.
- The dark-to-light transition feels intentional.
- Mobile priorities differ appropriately from desktop.
- Unsupported claims are absent.
- Product discovery works even with animation disabled.
- The page can be built from reusable components.
