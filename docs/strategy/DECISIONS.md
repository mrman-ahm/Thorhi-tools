# THROHI Website — Decision Log

**Date:** 2026-07-22

---

## D-001 — Legacy website role

**Decision:** Treat the current website as a legacy catalogue source, not as a design or technical reference.

**Reason:** It contains usable product names, categories, codes, images, and contact details, but its presentation and content quality are inconsistent.

**Consequence:** All migrated information requires validation and cleanup.

**Reversible:** No.

---

## D-002 — Visual benchmark

**Decision:** Use strong international medical, dental, surgical, and industrial product websites as quality references rather than local competitor aesthetics.

**Reason:** The target is an international standard, not imitation of the local market.

**Consequence:** Reference principles may come from world-class brands, but no layout or interaction will be copied.

**Reversible:** Yes, if the client later provides a specific approved reference.

---

## D-003 — Design direction

**Decision:** Combine Steel After Dark, Surgical Light, and Precision Archive.

**Reason:** This provides the visual impact the owner appears to expect while preserving catalogue usability and professional credibility.

**Consequence:** The hero and selected moments may be cinematic, while catalogue and form pages remain bright and functional.

**Reversible:** Yes, before high-fidelity approval.

---

## D-004 — Effects policy

**Decision:** Use one major hero effect and one secondary signature interaction.

**Reason:** Effects should demonstrate precision and product quality, not overwhelm navigation or performance.

**Consequence:** No heavy motion on catalogue, product, inquiry, or contact pages.

**Reversible:** Yes.

---

## D-005 — Brand colors

**Decision:** Preserve the logo's blue and green identity through refined, deeper website colors.

**Reason:** The owner explicitly requested use of logo colors, but the current bright gradients would feel dated if used everywhere.

**Consequence:** Final values require original logo artwork or better source files.

**Reversible:** Yes, before the design system is approved.

---

## D-006 — Conversion model

**Decision:** The website is inquiry-led rather than ecommerce-led.

**Reason:** The existing catalogue already uses “Add to Inquiry,” and the available context suggests B2B sourcing rather than retail checkout.

**Consequence:** Multi-product inquiry is a core system. Pricing, payment, and checkout are excluded from version one.

**Reversible:** Yes, if the client confirms retail requirements.

---

## D-007 — Catalogue structure

**Decision:** Keep four top-level divisions and regroup the large legacy category list into manageable product families.

**Reason:** The current category structure is too long and inconsistent for usable navigation.

**Consequence:** Every legacy product requires taxonomy mapping before import.

**Reversible:** Partially.

---

## D-008 — Product search priority

**Decision:** Make product-code and product-name search globally prominent.

**Reason:** Professional buyers may arrive knowing an exact code and should not be forced to navigate categories.

**Consequence:** Search appears in the header, homepage, and product areas.

**Reversible:** No.

---

## D-009 — Claims and compliance

**Decision:** Publish no manufacturing, certification, material, export, quality, or OEM claim without evidence and client approval.

**Reason:** The current public site contains generic claims and compliance imagery that have not been validated.

**Consequence:** Some company pages remain conditional until documents are supplied.

**Reversible:** No, except when evidence is received.

---

## D-010 — Logo handling

**Decision:** Request or reconstruct a high-quality logo and avoid extracting the red cross-like symbol for standalone use.

**Reason:** The supplied raster image is insufficient for production and the cross-like element may create legal or brand confusion.

**Consequence:** Final navigation, favicon, and social assets depend on approved logo work.

**Reversible:** Partially.

---

## D-011 — Precision Through Time

**Decision:** Retain “Precision Through Time” as an editorial brand experience, not as THROHI's unverified corporate history.

**Reason:** The concept is distinctive and relevant to instrument design, but must remain historically accurate.

**Consequence:** The homepage uses a condensed version and Resources may host the full experience.

**Reversible:** Yes.
